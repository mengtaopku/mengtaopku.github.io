Invested Capital相关概念

发表于 2018-09-08 | 更新于 2018-09-09 | 分类于 金融 | 评论数： | 阅读次数：
本文字数： 6.8k | 阅读时长 ≈ 11 分钟

IC/CE/TIC/TC

定义

Invested Capital在investopedia上有定义，摘录如下：

Invested capital is the total amount of money raised by a company by issuing securities to equity shareholders and debt to bondholders, where invested capital is calculated by adding the total debt and capital lease obligations to the amount of equity issued to investors. Invested capital is not a line item in company’s financial statement because debt, capital leases and stockholder’s equity are each listed separately in the balance sheet.

总结为公式是：
$$
Invested\;Capital=Debt+(Capital\; leases)+Equity
$$
区分 要与Asset相区分

我们认为，total capital，invested capital和total invested capital是一个概念，总资本、投资资本、总投资资本，都指公司总共投入多少钱。Total capital由付息债务debt和equity组成，这与Asset（由包括不付息债务的liability，如A/P，和equity组成）不同，Asset中的non-intereset current liability不是投入的资本。

细微差别

  • TIC

    概念来源于用EV/EBITDA来估值

    All else being equal, the justified EV/EBITDA based on fundamentals should be positively related to the expected growth rate in free cash flow to the firm, positively related to expected profitability as measured by return on invested capital, and negatively related to the business’s weighted average cost of capital.

    TIC与EV有很好关联性

    • 原版书对total invested capital有这样的定义：

    • 正文中 Total invested capital (TIC)/The sum of market value of common equity, book value of preferred equity, and face value of debt, sometimes also known as the market value of invested capital/The market value of debt and equity, that is an alternative to enterprise value. In contrast with EV, TIC includes the market value of equity and debt, but does not deduct cash and investments.

    • 术语表中 The sum of market value of common equity, book value of preferred equity, and face value of debt.
      • $Total\;Invested\;Capital=MV_{common\;equity}+BV_{preferred\; equity}+FC_{debt}$
    • 关联 Enterprise value = TIC - Cash&Investments
      • EV是强调MV的，当然当MV of debt无法获取时，也会使用BV
      • Cash and investments (sometimes termed nonearning assets/Cash and investments (specifically cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments)) are subtracted because EV is designed to measure the net price an acquirer would pay for the company as a whole.
    • 我们这样认为，TIC有时候指IC的MV，有时用FV/BV计算debt也行，其实这正表明这三个概念是一样的

  • IC

    概念来源于ROIC的定义

    Once future income statements and balance sheets are constructed, analysts can use them to determine the rate of return on invested capital (ROIC)（A measure of the after-tax profitability of the capital invested by the company’s shareholders and debt holders.） implied by their assumptions. ROIC measures the profitability of the capital invested by the company’s shareholders and debt holders.

    • The numerator for ROIC is usually net operating profit less adjusted taxes (NOPLAT). NOPLAT is basically earnings before interest expense (i.e., earnings available to provide a return to both equity holders and debt holders).

    • The denominator for ROIC is invested capital, which is calculated as operating assets less operating liabilities. Invested capital can be measured at the beginning of an accounting period or as an average of the beginning and end of the accounting period.

      这个定义讲述了IC主要是由$OA-OL$构成，可使用t=t，也可以用t=t和t+1的平均

    • ROIC is a better measure of profitability than return on equity because it is not affected by a company’s degree of financial leverage. In general, sustainably high ROIC is a sign of a competitive advantage.

    • To increase ROIC, a company must either increase earnings, reduce invested capital, or both.

    • 原版书中对invested capital这样定义：

      Hawawini and Viallet (2011 p. 628) define invested capital in detail as cash and cash equivalents plus working capital requirement (operating assets minus operating liabilities, where operating assets reflect receivables, inventories, and prepaid expenses and operating liabilities include payables and accrued expenses) plus net fixed assets.

      这一结论是容易理解的，IC，TIC，TC都是相似概念，推导的结果是TC的再加上cash

  • CE和ROCE

    • A closely related measure to ROIC, but focusing on pretax operating profit, is return on capital employed (ROCE)（Operating profit divided by capital employed (debt and equity capital).）, which is essentially ROIC before tax (This presentation follows one line of analyst practice. But some authorities treat ROCE as identical to ROIC). This measure is defined as operating profit divided by capital employed (debt and equity capital).

    • As a pretax measure, ROCE can be useful in several contexts, such as peer comparisons of companies in countries with different tax structures, because comparison of underlying profitability would not be biased in favor of companies benefitting from low tax rate regimes.

    • 可见，CE和那三个概念也无差别，下面是investopedia的定义：

      Capital employed, also known as funds employed, is the total amount of capital used for the acquisition of profits. It is the value of all the assets employed in a business, and can be calculated by adding fixed assetsto working capital or by subtracting current liabilities from total assets. By employing capital, you make an investment.

TC

$$
Total\; Capital=Debt+Equity-cash\\
=Liability-noninterest\;Current\;Liability+Equity-cash\\
=Asset-noninterest\;current\;liability-cash\\
=Current Asset(excluding cash) + noncurrent\;Asset-noninterest\;current\;liability\\
=working\;capital(adjusted)+noncurrent\;asset\\
\overset{不考虑无形资产，简化处理}{=}WC+FA
$$

  • 第一式为TC的定义式，在这里，我们将cash扣除，原因是cash不是投入，可以随时拿走

  • TC=WC+FA是重要结论，与Hawawini and Viallet的定义些许差别，原因在于一开始扣除了cash

  • 除了扣除cash，还要注意这里是不考虑无形资产的简化

  • $\Delta TC=\Delta WC+\Delta FC\overset{without disposal}{=}WCInv+FCInv-DeprExpense$

  • Investment in fixed capital in excess of depreciation (also called “net new investment in fixed capital”)，也被称作incremental FCInv. This method involves a simplification because it considers depreciation as the only noncash charge, so the method does not work well when that approximation is not a good assumption. (FCFF或FCFE的改写的简化处理，从$\Delta TC$角度去理解FCF)

  • TC的概念在原版书中出现是介绍EVA（经济附加值）

    A commercial implementations of the residual income concept is economic value added (EVA)，可以看成是整个公司赚到的超额收益，是经济economic运作带来的经济增加值，超过机会成本的部分。
    $$
    EVA=NOPAT-WACC\times TC\\
    =EBIT\times(1-T)-$WACC
    $$

    对比 由于MV变化导致的value added叫MVA（marked value added）

    $$MVA=MV_{company}-Accounting\;BV\;of\;TC$$

    A company that generates positive economic profit should have a market value in excess of the accounting book value of its capital.

    Total capital = net working capital + net fixed assets = book value of long-term debt + book value of equity

EE

在对非上市公司估值时，有特殊的剩余收益法（RI），也被称作excess earnings method(EE)。EE其实就是指无形资产产生的收益。

$$EE=firm\;earnings-earnings\;required\;to\;provide\;the\;required\;rate\;of\;return\;on\;WC\;and\;FC$$

原理：

$$Firm\;Value=TC=IC=E+D=WC+FA+IA$$

  • 逐项分析：

WC：流动资产和流动负债，其账面价值与市场价值是很接近的，最简单的情况就是直接用BV作为WC的市场价值

FA：固定资产，有形资产，如房子、设备，在市场上找类似的房子、设备就可以估出来

IA：最难估算，没有物理形态，在面上难以找出可比对象

  • 解决方法

Total return来源于WC, FC和IA三个部分，将前两者的收益扣除后就得到IA当年带来的回报，将其视作IA当年产生的CF，然后用GGM即可估值IA

最后，$Equity\;Value=FV-MV_D$
