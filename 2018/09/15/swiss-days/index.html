How to operate the Solid-Gas Interface Chamber

发表于 2018-09-15 | 更新于 2018-09-16 | 分类于 化学 | 评论数： | 阅读次数：
本文字数： 14k | 阅读时长 ≈ 23 分钟

by Luca

Introduce a sample/manipulator in the load lock and analysis chamber

  • The load lock should be vented and the shutter (V1) should be closed. Check that
    there is no manipulator installed.

    Figure 1. Control panel of the manipulator and load lock

  • Close the venting valve (control panel of the manipulator, V4, close position).

  • Install the manipulator (pay attention to the cooling circuit of the cryo-holder and to the connections of the heated sample holder). Check the orientation of the sample: the sample/sample holder must be on the beamline (photons) side. The manipulator slides easily until the last part, where its head has to go through o-rings. Push the manipulator gently until its flange is in contact with that of the W motor and fix it with the two M5 screws available (IMPORTANT: it is not necessary to overtight the screws!). Connect the ground using the yellow/green cable and the clamp available (see Figure 2).

    Figure 2. Heated sample holder correctly installed.

  • Cryo-holder. With the manipulator in position, be sure that the cooling coil is inside the dewar, then connect:

    i) the differential pumping (KF16 bellow going to the HiCube pumping station)

    ii) the power and temperature sensor cables

    iii) the cooling circuit (the 6 mm tube + Swagelok from the mass flow controller of nitrogen connects to the cooling coil of the manipulator).

  • Open the evacuation restricted (V2 – root pump, pumping down through a needle valve).

  • Wait until the pressure goes below 50 mbar (check the Kurt J Lesker pressure gauge on the beamline side – it usually takes 1-2 minutes).

  • Close the V2 and open the V3 (evacuation full – root pump full pumping).

  • Wait until the pressure goes below 0.3 mbar (should be quite fast – ca. 1 minute).

  • Close the V3 and open the shutter (V1). The pressure in the experimental chamber will increase to low 10-4/high 10-5 mbar (you can check it in the beamline control panel “ES1-MP1” in the “X07DB NanoXAS Endstation” window), then will decrease in a few minutes to low 10-6 mbar/10-7 mbar.

  • Move the switch “Sample” of the control panel to “In”. The z coarse motor will start to move and the manipulator will approach the ambient pressure (AP) cell. The motor stops automatically when the sample is inside the AP cell (limit switch).

Align the samples

  • Open the Labview .vi called “Chamber control” (or select it from the toolbar of Windows). When a new sample is introduced from the load lock the X, Y and Z positions of the manipulator are 0.000, 0.000 and 1.000, respectively. Activate the motors checking the button “Stoped”. The motors are off (red). Check each of them to activate the motors (they become green).

  • In the SES software cascade menu, check “calibration”, then “voltages”. A window opens, displaying the integrated spectrum (snapshot mode, counts per second) from the detector image in a selected kinetic energy window. The kinetic energy of the analyzer can be entered manually, and is usually that of a reference, intense photoemission peak (roughly: KE = hv(selected in the monochromator) – BE). The width of the kinetic energy window depends on the selected pass energy and is ca. 1/10 of it (e.g. with 50 eV PE the width is ca. 5 eV, +- 2.5 around the selected KE). If the counts are not enough and/or the width of the KE window is not enough to display a peak, the PE can be modified. Be sure that the PE value respects the allowed energy range. Click “View”, then “Energy range” and check that the selected PE falls in a valid KE interval.

    Figure 3. “Calibration”$\rightarrow$”Voltages” window displaying an intense peak and its integrated area during the alignment.

  • Check “View”, then “Area”. A second window opens showing the integrated counts per second underneath the peak (see Figure 3).

  • Apply the voltage to the detector and screen (release the red control button of the analyzer). Be sure that the control units of the analyzer are on and that the two buttons in the front panel are in the “on” position (otherwise no voltage will be applied).

  • There should not be any peak at the beginning (only white noise from the MCP).

  • Start to approach the manipulator to the cone of the analyzer (the usual working distance depends on the manipulator used and has to be asked to an experienced person when the beamtime starts. To approach, move the Z of the manipulator while controlling the display of the endoscope camera. Steps of 1.0 mm can be used for the coarse approach. When the sample is aprox. 0.5 mm far from the focal distance start to move by 0.1 mm steps. Some counts should appear, then a peak should develop. If the window displays only background the kinetic energy can be moved until the peak is displayed. Maximize the countrate by moving the Z in steps of 0.05 mm.

  • When the countrate is maximized, it is possible to start to measure the spectra.

  • To move to a fresh sample spot always check the contrate (“calibration”, then “voltages” in the SES software). Move preferentially the Y. Moving the Y should not imply moving the Z to refocus (the sample to aperture distance should not focus).

  • In case the X has to be moved, retract the Z (going toward more positive X values will approach the sample surface to the cone of the analyzer $\rightarrow$ COLLISION DANGER!), then move the X and re-maximize the counts moving the Z while controlling the countrate on a intense peak.

Start to measure XPS

  • With the SES software open, check “Sequence” $\rightarrow$ ”Setup”. A window opens, displaying the sequence of spectra.

    Figure 4. Sequence of spectra (selected – green – and unselected – red -).

  • You can either select a specific spectral region (already present in the sequence) or edit a new one. To select a present region check the box on the extreme left of the region (from redi t will become green).

    The easiest way to edit a new region is to copy an old one (just highlight an old spectral region and check the button “Copy”. An identical spectral region will be generated.

  • To modify/edit a spectral region double click on it will open the spectral region editor (Figure 5). Unless angular lens mode is required, the usual operation lens mode is “T_HiPPHAXPES” (Don’t modify the lens mode!). It is possible to modify the pass energy (always check that pass energy falls in an allowed kinetic energy range!), the number of iterations (number of sweeps of the selected spectral region acquired within an iteration – an iteration contains ALL the spectral regions selected in the “Sequence”$\rightarrow$”Setup” window), the kinetic energy width (editing either the start and end kinetic energy or moving the center of the window), dwell time (usually 0.12 s, the dwell time steps have fixed values, depending on the frame rate of the detector CCD camera), and step size (usually 50-100 meV for high resolution spectra and 250 meV for Survey scans).

    Figure 5. Spectral region editor, containing all the parameters of the spectrum.

  • Check all the spectral region that you want to acquire within an iteration. The “Setup” window contains a box with “Number of iterations” (see Figure 4). Unless modified, this number is set to 100. It can be reduced/increased while acquiring the spectra. 1 iteration may contain a single sweep per spectral region of spectral regions acquired more sweeps (usually weak and noisy signals need to be acquired more sweeps per iteration).

  • Be sure that the beamline shutter is open and that the voltage is applied to the detector and screen (red buttons on the table).

  • Start to acquire checking “Start” (setup window) or “Sequence”à”Run” from the main SES window.

  • The number of iterations can be modified at any time checking “Sequence”à”Number of Sweeps” from the main menu of SES. Modify the green number on top of the window clicking on the box “Tot” and editing the new number of iterations.

    Figure 6. Window to modify the number of iterations while acquiring XPS.

Measure XPS at ambient pressure (mbar range)

  • (If the .vi is not found in the toolbar) Open the “Chamber-Data-to-file” Labview .vi (Desktop). Check the green button “Start Charts”. This .vi saves all the SGIC parameters (pressures from the Baratrons, temperature of the sample, vapor pressure of the liquid reservoir, etc.). A second .vi, named “Chamber-Chart” automatically opens. With this .vi one can display in real time 4 parameters of the SGIC. Check the 4 rectangular boxes up left and select the parameter to display (pressure, temperature, etc.). Checking the smaller box containing the line it is possible to modify the line style and to select the Y scale (two Y scales are available – it is important to select different Y scales for parameter channels having extremely different absolute values, e.g. pressure and temperature). Activate the .vi checking the white arrow. The parameters will be displayed with time.

  • Close the KF16 bypass valve connecting the ambient pressure cell (upstream, Figure 7) with the high vacuum chamber (IMPORTANT! This insulates the experimental cell).


    Figure 7. Bypass valve (closed, gas/gas mixtures can be dosed).

  • Open the last on-off valve connecting the dosing lines with the ambient pressure cell (red and blue valve with 6 mm Swagelok connectors, see Figure 8).


    Figure 8. Last valve connecting the gas dosing systems to the ambient pressure cell. In the photo it is open (gas can be dosed in the cell).

  • To dose incondensable gases (upper gas line):

    i) start the software controlling the mass flow controllers (mfc). Double click on “FlowDDE”, a communication window appears, check “communication”à”Open communications”, the window should display “Connection to FLOW-BUS interface OK”. Double click on “FlowView” icon, one window controlling the mfc will open. To select the right channel (right gas to dose) check the arrow of “Channel” until the number corresponding to the gas appears (it takes a while to switch from a number to another).

    ii) close the valve to the HiCube pump (black on-off valve at the end of the gas line)

    iii) open the valve connecting the incondesable gas line with the ambient pressure cell (black on-off valve, Figure 9)


    Figure 9. Gas line for incondensable gases. Right configuration to dose gas/gas mixture. Valve to the HiCube closed and valve to the AP Cell open.

    iv) open the mfc using the window controlling it (“FlowView”): one can either input the mln/min value (down left) or scroll the arrow up to the desired value. Use a small flow (e.g. 1 sccm) and check that the pressure in the ambient pressure cell increases (“Chamber-Chart” .vi)

    v) open the KF25 diaphragm valve downstream the ambient pressure cell flow tube. The pressure in the cell should decrease.


    10. KF25 diaphragm valve downstream the flow tube (to tune the pressure in the AP Cell while dosing gas/gas mixture).

    vi) play with the mfc controller and the KF25 diaphragm valve to get the desired gas flow in the cell while keeping the pressure stable (e.g. at 1.0 mbar).

  • To dose vapors:

    i) before starting to dose, activate the .vi “Cooler-1” or “Cooler-2”, which control the temperature of the Peltier element. Be sure that the control unit of the Peltier element (below the vapor generators) is on and that the cooling water to the Peltier is running (flow meter connected to the cooler). Activate the “Cooler-x” .vi and set the desired temperature in the “Setpoint” box. The cooler will start to cool/warm up the vial (check the led “Heating out” and “Cooling out”.

    Activate the “Vapor-Pressure-x” .vi (corresponding to the Cooler-x), which reads a pressure gauge in the gas line of the Cooler-x. Check “Zeroing”, the “Vapor-Pressure x” should go to zero.

    ii) check that the valves connecting the vapor generators (1 and 2) to the AP Cell are closed

    iii) in the vapor generator containing the compound to dose, close the valve connecting it to the HiCube pump

    iv) open the valve to the glass vial containing the liquid. The pressure of the vapors in equilibrium with the liquid at the temperature set by the cooler can be read in the “Vapor-Pressure-x” .vi (see Figure 11).

    v) open the valve connecting the cooler to the AP Cell. The pressure of the AP Cell will increase. Tune it with the KF25 diaphragm valve downstream the flow tube (Figure 10).


Figure 11. Cooler 1 (in the configuration shown here vapors are not dosed in the AP Cell. The vial is closed and the gas line is pumped down with the HiCube pump.

Remove/Replace a sample/manipulator from the AP Cell

  • Close the valve to the dosing systems and the KF25 diaphragm valve to the root pump downstream the flow tube of the AP Cell (Figure 8 and 10, respectively). The AP Cell is now pumped by the cone of the analyzer. The pressure should decrease.
  • Close the valve connecting the incondensable gas line/vapor dosing line to the AP Cell.
  • Wait until the pressure in the AP Cell is at least in the 10-2 mbar range.
  • Open (SLOWLY) the bypass (Figure 7) while checking the pressure in the main chamber. The pressure will increase (usually it is in the low 10-7 range), then decrease again (the speed depends on the gas dosed, e.g. i twill decrease slowly after dosing water for long time).
  • Move the manipulator to the transfer positions, which are X = 0.000 mm, Y = 0.000 mm and Z = 1.000 mm.
  • Retract the manipulator from the AP Cell to the load lock moving the “Sample” button of the control unit (see Figure 1) to “Out”. The motor moving the manipulator will start and move the manipulator back to the load lock position (it stops automatically).
  • To vent the load lock and replace the sample/manipulator:

i) close the shutter valve (V1)

ii) remove the ground and unscrew the two M5 screws fixing the flange of the manipualtor to that of the W motor.

iii) open the venting valve (V4). Nitrogen (or air) vents the load lock.

iv) check the pressure of the Kurt J Lesker gauge connected to the load lock, from 0.0000 will raise to 1000 mbar.

v) when the pressure is 1 bar remove the manipualtor (pull and rotate gently to facilitate the sliding)

vi) remove the manipualtor and replace it/replace the sample.
