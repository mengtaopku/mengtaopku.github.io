Research skills and areas

Now I am a Ph.D. candidate of Physical Chemistry/Heterogeneous Catalysis in Peking University. In this area, I have gained lots of skills:

  • Be familiar with reaction pathways of heterogeneous catalysis, especially reverse water-gas shift (RWGS) reaction and Fischer-Tropsch synthesis (FTS).
  • Be familiar with normal XPS experiments, quasi in-suit XPS experiments, AP-XPS (Ambient pressure XPS) and the data processing with CasaXPS and Igor Pro Softwares.
  • Be familiar with transient kinetic analysis (TKA) and steady-state isotopic transient kinetic analysis (SSITKA) through Mass spectrum (MS) and data processing with QUADERA software.

There is some work I have done until now:

  • Do a lot of research in CO2 selective reduction to CO, also known as RWGS reaction, which provides a carbon neutral cycle loop to generate chemicals and fuels via hydrogen being available from nuclear and renewable energy.
  • Design and set up a SSITKA apparatus for investigation on the mechanism of many gas-solid reaction processes.
  • Develop the most active catalysts for RWGS reaction with low cost and high thermal stability in reducible atmosphere.
